Data Analysis of National Day Holiday Interview Questionnaire Results

Q1: How likely are you to try new leisure activities next time?

Analysis: Most respondents (80%) indicated they are likely or very likely to try new leisure activities, showing strong willingness to explore.

Q2: Which information channel do you rely on most?

Analysis: Social media is the primary information channel (40%), followed by e-commerce platforms (20%), with other channels showing relatively even distribution.

Q3: Approximate total spending during the holiday?

Analysis: Most respondents (30%) spent under ¥500, with spending distribution showing a clear long-tail pattern.

👥 Q4: What is your current status?

💡 Insight: Employed individuals account for half (50%) of respondents, while freelancers/self-employed make up 30%, indicating the survey population is primarily working professionals.

🚗 Q5: What was your main mode of transportation? (by age group)

🚄 Key Findings:
  • Self-driving dominates across all age groups
  • 26-35 age group are the primary users of high-speed rail (50%)
  • 30% of the 45+ age group did not travel
  • Younger groups (Under 18, 19-25) show 100% self-driving usage

⭐ Q6: Overall satisfaction level

🎉 Outstanding Performance: Satisfaction reaches 100%, with 70% being "Very Satisfied" and 30% "Satisfied" - an exceptionally high level in consumer research with no neutral or dissatisfied responses.

📈 Demographic Overview

🔍 Population Insights: The survey captures a diverse age distribution with strong representation from 26-35 and 36-45 age groups, combined with high employment rates and exceptional satisfaction levels.

🔍 Key Findings Summary

Main Discoveries:

  • Transportation Preferences: Self-driving is the primary mode across all age groups, with high-speed rail particularly popular among 26-35 year olds (50%)
  • Satisfaction Levels: Exceptional overall satisfaction with 100% of respondents reporting satisfied or very satisfied
  • Demographic Profile: Respondents are primarily employed (50%) and freelancers/self-employed (30%)
  • Age Distribution: Balanced representation across age groups with 26-35 being the largest segment (30%)

Notable Observations:

  • 30% of the 45+ age group did not travel, possibly indicating lower travel needs in this demographic
  • All respondents expressed satisfaction with no negative feedback - rare in typical surveys
  • High-speed rail usage peaks at 50% in the 26-35 age group, significantly higher than other transportation modes
  • Younger demographics (under 25) rely exclusively on self-driving

Interview Data Analysis — Affinity Diagram

Digital affinity diagram converted from interview responses. Contains 30+ sticky notes, 5 main themes and 15 subthemes.

1. Raw Data

The interview content includes three main themes and about 100 responses used as raw data.

  • Theme 1: Holiday Planning & Decision-Making
  • Theme 2: Actual Experiences & Interactions During the Holiday
  • Theme 3: Feelings, Reflection & Future Expectations

2. Synthesis

We conducted thematic clustering (affinity analysis) and organized content into five hierarchical levels:

Raw Data → Sticky Notes (Keywords) → Subthemes → Themes → Core Insights

3. Digital Affinity Diagram

Final diagram contains 30+ sticky notes, 5 main themes and 15 subthemes. Below are the themes and notes (sticky items):

Theme 1 — Planning Triggers & Motivations

Timing of Planning

  • Planned 1 month before to relax after work.
  • Planned 2 weeks before after roommates discussed trips.
  • Planned just 3 days before—wanted to stay home.
  • Planned early to visit exhibitions or take photos.

Motivations

  • Desire to relax and escape from work pressure.
  • Interest in cultural/art events.
  • Family bonding and shared meals.
  • Personal hobbies (photography, reading, dancing).

Theme 2 — Decision Factors

Practical Considerations

  • Cost and distance were main concerns.
  • Transportation convenience mattered most.
  • Accommodation availability and weather conditions.

Personal Priorities

  • Avoiding crowds and ensuring rest time.
  • Family preferences over personal desires.
  • Health condition influencing activity type.

Theme 3 — Information Sources & Planning Tools

Social Media & Apps

  • Xiaohongshu and Douyin for travel ideas.
  • Ctrip for hotel and flight booking.
  • Art gallery websites and Douban groups for event info.

Social Networks

  • Friends' and family's experiences influenced decisions.
  • Teachers, colleagues, and customers offered advice.

Theme 4 — Holiday Experiences & Challenges

Memorable Moments

  • Watching sunrise, beach walks, and square dancing.
  • Family meals and successful pastry sales.
  • Finishing yoga poses or seeing art exhibitions.

Unexpected Events

  • Lost direction → used Baidu Maps.
  • Car problem → rental service fixed it.
  • Muscle ache → booked home massage online.

Technology Support

  • Meituan for ingredient delivery.
  • Ctrip and Didi for smooth travel.
  • WeChat for coordination and communication.

Theme 5 — Reflection & Future Expectations

Overall Feelings

  • Most felt relaxed, satisfied, or inspired.
  • Some felt tired but fulfilled.

Plans for Next Year

  • Plan earlier and check logistics.
  • Try new but similar activities (new bookstores, new recipes).

Desired "Holiday Assistant"

  • Recommend books or destinations.
  • Predict crowds and weather.
  • Manage yoga schedule and ingredient supply.

4. Insights & Reflection

  1. Motivation is mainly relaxation- and interest-driven, not purely tourism-based.
  2. Decisions depend most on cost, convenience, and crowd levels.
  3. Social media and personal networks are key information sources.
  4. Experiences are mostly positive, with technology tools enhancing convenience.
  5. Participants express a growing desire for personalized, organized, and intelligent holiday planning in the future.

Unexpected findings

  • "Non-travel" holidays (reading, baking, yoga) were surprisingly popular, showing a preference for recovery over exploration.
  • Occupation and age clearly influence holiday style.
  • Many wanted a "Holiday Assistant," suggesting a potential design opportunity for AI-based recommendation tools.
